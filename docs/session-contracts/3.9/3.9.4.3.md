## Session 3.9.4.3 — Neon recovery posture & drill

**Sub-version:** 3.9.4.3 (Phase 4 — Continuity & recovery; one session)
**Master plan:** `docs/VERSION_3_9_PLAN.md` §3.9.4.3
**UX gate:** No

## 1. Objective

The database's actual restore capability is known, documented, and exercised
once.

## 2. Current context and dependencies

- No dependencies; schedules freely within Phase 4. Never touches production
  state (the drill uses a disposable branch).

## 3. Done conditions

- The posture is recorded from the live plan (PITR window, branch-restore
  semantics, what the current tier actually guarantees).
- One drill executes: restore a point-in-time branch, run the app's
  verification against it (migrations status, row-count sanity on
  system-of-record tables — identity, tokens, durable app data), and tear it
  down.
- The runbook states the from-loss recovery sequence and the
  regenerable-vs-authoritative split (what must restore vs. what re-syncs from
  ESI/SDE).

## 4. In scope

Posture doc, one branch-restore drill, runbook.

## 5. Out of scope

- Plan/tier changes (report if the posture is insufficient — Ryan decides).
- Any production mutation.

## 6. Hard constraints

- Arc-wide (Phase 4): the drill runs against a disposable Neon branch, never
  production state; secrets never enter the runbook (locations only); the
  drill produces a written runbook + a timed result in SCRATCHPAD.
- The `neon` CLI is the tool of record; destructive/branch operations are
  confirmed with Ryan before execution.

## 7. Decisions the session plan must resolve

- The restore point and verification query set for the drill.
- Where the runbook lives (backed-up workspace home per 3.9.4.2).

## 8. Acceptance criteria

- The recorded posture matches the live Neon plan's actual guarantees.
- The drill readout shows the restored branch passing migrations-status and
  row-count sanity, then torn down.
- The runbook covers the from-loss sequence and the
  regenerable-vs-authoritative split.

## 9. Verification

- The drill readout (commands + verification results) and the committed
  runbook.
- SCRATCHPAD records the timed result.
- Changelog entry + APP_VERSION bump (3.9.4.3) for tracked changes; otherwise
  the non-code completion shape.
